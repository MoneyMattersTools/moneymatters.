Frequently asked questions

Why do I need to make a copy of the sheet?

Your copy is private and yours alone. The master template stays clean for everyone, and you can edit, save, and revisit your version anytime — without affecting anyone else.

Can I share my copy with my spouse or partner?

Yes. Once you have your copy, you can share it with anyone in your household just like any Google Sheet.

Do you offer refunds?

If a tool isn’t what you expected, contact us within 14 days for a full refund.

Will the tools get updates?

Yes. We periodically improve the tools and notify buyers of significant updates. You can request the updated version anytime.

Is this financial advice?

No. MoneyMatters tools are educational and for your personal planning. For decisions with major consequences, please consult a licensed financial professional.

How is this different from apps like Mint or YNAB?

Those are subscription apps with locked-down features and your data in their hands. Our tools are spreadsheets you fully own and can adapt — no recurring fees, no lock-in.

Built on real principles

Our tools are grounded in widely-accepted personal finance frameworks: zero-based budgeting, the 4% safe withdrawal rule for retirement, compound-interest modeling for long-term planning, and standard net-worth accounting.

Where we make assumptions (like return rates or inflation), we tell you what they are and let you adjust them. No black boxes.
